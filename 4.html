I have one thing to tell you. Now I don't even know basic codes,
actually I was a high school regional representative at the programming Olympiad.

I can recall somethings while studying that I had forgotten, one of them is the data type.
When I was young, it was so annoying to process differently depending on the data type.
In fact, many of the errors that come out when debugging were as above.
Adding code carefully is not a difficult process,
but it was too boring. For us who study algorithms for ease of computation,
the above process is rather troublesome. That’s why I hated.

There are difference between 1 and “1”.
1+1 is 2, but "1"+"1" is "11".
It does not seem important in the above example,
but if you confuse "1" with 1 it will lead to a big misunderstanding later.
In addition, you will not know what we are trying to represent.

If we had input (milk, 250),
how do computer know if this is 250 bottles of milk or 250ml of milk?
You can think of it as reducing misunderstanding like above.

Also, what if you put two 250ml bottles together?
You can call it 500ml by doing 250ml+250ml.
One milk bottle plus other one can be called two bottles. But we can express our intentions by saying,
"How many bottles do you have?" and "How many milliliters?"
In this way, we can make sure what it means to compute or represent.

The same is true of everyday life.
To explain exactly what we're trying to say,
it should be stated the words are in 'which field' and 'what meaning' they contain.
There are many meanings in the word 'contain' the above sentence.
For this, it is neat to define it every time.
But it can’t because of its efficiency.
And misunderstandings are repeated.

We can't explain definition for every time to say it.
Therefore, we should always keep in mind the possibility of misunderstanding.
It should be acceptable in an open position. But many people can't.
They don't even recognize misunderstandings that arise from different definitions.
They don't even seem to know that fact it can be different.
Even the word "fact" that I said 10 minutes ago and the meaning of the word "fact"
that I just said can be subtly different.
Also, you won't know accurately 100 percent without my explanation the word "fact" that I used right above.

Computer language has clearly distinguished as if warning us from such a thing.
Something with defined should only be used as defined.
If there is a change, it should be marked and used as it has been.
Anyone who has spoken to many people will know. Rather,
this kind of approach may be more efficient.

It's up to you to judge. I’ve been bothered by the strict distinction.
But as I grew up, I realized the importance of it.
On the way rather, I was eroded by its importance and overreacted.
Now I know it's different, and I just accept it. In a bad way, it's only 90 percent communication, not 100 percent.

But I'm still communicating in this way.
