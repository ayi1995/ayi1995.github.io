2. Practice

Have you set a goal to acquire basic knowledge? Then you have to “do it” now.
There are many ways to acquire knowledge, but I distinguish it in two main ways.
Practice (external learning) and thinking (internal learning).
As you can see from the expression, 'practice' is that focuses on utilizing
what I have learned and 'thinking' is that focuses on returning, memorizing and exploring what I have learned.

Knowledge like language is that needs practice.
If you want to be good at Korean, you should meet many Korean speakers and talk.
If you are reading the Korean dictionary, then you won't get much help in your Korean.
Of course, if you want to study Korean because you are curious about it,
it is necessary to think about it, but in this case, it is desirable to view it as a study rather than a language.

It's like big data. Experience countless cases, and naturally find the best response.
It is ridiculous to memorize and explore the number of all cases in the use of the same or different language in some cases.
That is, we have a lot of experience to say that we are using big data in our head.

Readers who read the first chapter would have recognized the strangeness.
You will have the same question as “Isn’t writer said the realization of intelligence through the use of big data is different from human intelligence?”
That's right, but we can't talk about it yet.

(Amazingly, machine learning can also produce results similar to forgetfulness
by intentionally making the weight of meaningless elements zero.

The importance of language is greater than thought.
Naturally, in order to acquire learning, one or more languages must be proficient.
If you don't know at least Korean, how can you read?
And the study that can be acquired in Korean is proportional to the number of books (information) written in Korean.
In other words, more knowledge can be accessed by using languages which many people uses and writes.

Then what language should we learn? Isn't English the universal language of course?
However, only half of them are right. There are many people who cannot speak English.
However, no one is bad at math. You gave up math? No, mathematics is not just a term for calculus.
Buy things, get change, buy three and triple the price.
This 'ability to write an expression' is called a mathematical ability.

The computer language that is commonly known is actually mathematics itself,
and therefore computer language is equivalent to the world's common language.
That's why when it comes to learning a computer language,
we don't have to worry about the meaning and mechanism of operation such as
“public/class/static/void/main/string/println”, but we have to run the program once by copying and pasting it,
and learn the language by correcting and debugging.

That's why in learning computer languages,
we need to learn the language by running a program even by copying and pasting,
correcting faulty grammar through compilation and debug,
rather than considering the meaning and mechanism of operation of public/class/static/void/string/println.
And then you will know the meaning one by one.
