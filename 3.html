3. Metacognition

There's something my boss said.

Leaders should not be afraid of failure.
Real failure is learning nothing form failure.
A more serious failure is not taking full risk for of failing.

I think so, too. You shouldn't be afraid of failure, and you must learn something from it.
In order to do so, there is one prerequisite. You must have to challenge with a basis.
And if it failed, you must find out why you failed.
If there are something to learn from it, you failed in the challenge, but you succeeded in learning.

Whether or not you know the reason is so important.
I failed three things today.

Class files were not well created in the '5-1. Create Desktop Applications' lecture.
I can't open the Raspberry Pi's kernel in the "5-2 Control of Things with Java" lecture.
And I failed to run virtual device in the '5-3 Create Android Applications' lecture.

I made right class file by re-running the program.
But I still don't know why it wasn't made well.
However, I learned that I should pay more attention to the production process.

The reason that I think "why I failed to open the kernel of RaspberryPi" is because there are no device.
These basic problems, especially hardware, are not easy to figure out.
The lecture deals with software and this is so basic hardware question that it is unclear how to ask.
There are no place to explain it because it is considered natural.

I was looking for a reason why I couldn't run the virtual device, but I quit.
Apparently, it was executed in the build window,
but the error "Android studio emulator was killed" keeps popping up.
It was said that it was a problem with environmental variables and disk capacity,
but it is not important now.

In general, we think that wrong made on purpose are heavier.
However, from a Buddhist point of view, they deal more heavily with the wrong they have made without knowing.
This is because mistakes can be repeated over and over again.
It is the story of whether it can be fixed or not.

Boostcourse didn't gave the answer to me about the third question.
I turn on it several times, no explanation of environmental variables and disk capacity was given.
Nevertheless, the first question was learned from the previous lecture,
I written in the comments about second question.
And I still don't know the answer to the third question, but it let me know that I didn't know.
It provided an opportunity to use metacognition capabilities appropriately.

In that respect, the Boostcourse is excellent.
Of course not everyone using the Boostcourse will do this intellectual activity.
As mentioned in the second article, sometimes it is also more important to get what you want quickly and use it.
But at the same time, what if we use metacognition?
Wouldn't it show the speed of learning like artificial intelligence?

Come on and study with me at BoostCourse.

The goal of writing is to interest people like me.
My "goal to take a lecture" is to become an "algorithm engineer."
In other words, I have to think about algorithms and engineering too.
You may also want to learn something through language.
Someone studies English and science separately, but I acquire scientific knowledge in English.
It may be slow, but there will definitely be a lot to gain.
There will be a variety of things to think about, and it will be hard but not boring.
