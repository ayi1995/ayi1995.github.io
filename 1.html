1. the Importance of a Goal

"Write once, run anywhere" Java was created with the above goals.
And I have a goal of giving you an interest in the language Java and Computer Science.

A goal brings many things. This GitHub, the space where I am writing now, is also the result of setting goals.
Many people would never have imagined that I could create a GitHub blog.
Because I created a GitHub account a day before the opening of the GitHub blog.
Blog quality is still not good, but anyway I made it!

You might think, "How does this article, which is more like a diary than a lecture review makes interest you?"
You may wonder what kind of support this is because it handles a very small part of the lecture
(only one sentence to exaggerate).

But people who don't have any information on the coding I've seen are terrified just by the word Java.
From that point of view, my article can give readers the impression that they are reading a coding-themed diary (or novel)
and if exposed to coding through it, they will naturally become curious.
Of course, I think it's true support that makes people who are not interested more interested than those who are already interested.

Think about artificial intelligence. These days, artificial intelligence is solidifying algorithms based on deep learning.
If someone asks me what the algorithm is, I'll answer 'how to achieve goal.'
This is because the solution we found to solve a particular problem is called an algorithm.
In other words, there must be a goal to come up with an algorithm.

If this was implemented with a particular algorithm, this means that there are some objectives(A).
In other words, A is needed to make intelligence.
To enlarge this, attempts to implement intelligence through algorithms come from the idea that our reason is likely to have been created for some purpose.

However, artificial intelligence, currently implemented in the above method, is still only a statistical tool.
The number of cases can be estimated without statistics, but it is just a product of mathematics.
Strong artificial intelligence may be implemented, but it seems far from being similar to "human."
Of course, there are no disagreement with the methodology of implementing something transcendence human beings.

But I think artificial intelligence will be implemented differently.
In particular, I believe that intelligence similar to 'human' will be implemented differently.
Through this, there is a question to answer, I have a goal.
I would like to first prove the existence of the question "the reason of our life,"
which precedes "the reason of our life(think, reason, else)."

There was an experiment with a small creature which has only 1,000 neurons called Caenorhabditis elegans.
We only implemented a network of random neurons in this small creature.
And there are what what seemed to be intelligence was implemented.
I think human intelligence has also been implemented through this process.
I think it is more likely that accidental connections have created reason so far.

However, even if the seed of all of our concerns above is "accidental", it is very important to set a goal.
Surprisingly, even the fact that “there is no goal” are found by the “goal” of revealing it.

I'm studying to see if artificial intelligence is implemented only physically.
Whether intelligence will be created solely by the supply of electric current
without the installation of software on very complex semiconductors.
Through this, I want to prove the existence of reason for life.
However, the process will definitely require 'artificial intelligence implemented by algorithms(software)'.
If my writing has stimulated your curiosity enough, I hope you will acquire basic knowledge in the BoostCourse.
